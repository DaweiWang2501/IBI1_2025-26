# Practical 13: Sequence comparison of human and mouse DLX5

## Online UniProt/BLAST findings

- Human DLX5 protein: **289 amino acids**.
- Subcellular localisation: mainly **nucleus**; database annotations also list **chromatin** and **cytoplasm**.
- Mouse DLX5 UniProt ID used for comparison: **P70396**.
- The practical handout screenshot reports the mouse DLX5 BLAST alignment as **90.1% identity**, score **1097**, E-value **3.4e-152**. When using the current canonical FASTA sequences downloaded for the Python analysis, the full-length non-gapped identity is higher, because the two 289-aa sequences differ at only 10 positions.
- **To verify before submission:** after running UniProt BLAST with “Restrict by taxonomy = Eutheria (placentals)”, copy the exact number of hits and the full percentage-identity range from the current UniProt results page here. These values can change when UniProt updates its database.

## Python analysis method

I implemented a simple pairwise **non-gapped global alignment**. The program compares sequences from amino acid 1 to the final amino acid, scores each aligned amino-acid pair with **BLOSUM62**, sums the scores, and calculates percentage identity.

A reproducible random protein sequence of length 289 was generated with random seed 13.

## Python results

| Comparison | Length | BLOSUM62 score | Score per residue | Identical residues | Percentage identity |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Human DLX5 vs mouse DLX5 | 289 | 1490 | 5.156 | 279/289 | 96.54% |
| Human DLX5 vs random protein | 289 | -365 | -1.263 | 9/289 | 3.11% |
| Mouse DLX5 vs random protein | 289 | -361 | -1.249 | 10/289 | 3.46% |

## Interpretation

The human and mouse DLX5 sequences are very closely related. Their BLOSUM62 score is strongly positive, and 279 of 289 amino acids are identical in the current canonical sequences used here. In contrast, both comparisons against the random protein give negative BLOSUM62 scores and very low percentage identity, close to what would be expected by chance for a random amino-acid sequence.

Therefore, **human DLX5 and mouse DLX5 are the most closely related pair** among the three comparisons. This is biologically reasonable because the two proteins are orthologues from mammals and perform conserved developmental/transcription-factor functions, whereas the random sequence has no expected evolutionary relationship to either protein.
